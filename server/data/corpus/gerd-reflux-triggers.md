# GERD and Acid Reflux Dietary Triggers

Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) occurs when stomach acid flows back into the esophagus. Certain foods and preparation styles relax the lower esophageal sphincter or increase acid exposure, worsening heartburn and regurgitation.

## Fatty and Fried Foods

High-fat and deep-fried foods delay gastric emptying and relax the lower esophageal sphincter, prolonging acid exposure. Fried chicken, french fries, and creamy or greasy dishes are common reflux triggers and tend to score high for GERD.

## Spicy Foods

Spicy dishes containing chili, hot sauce, or large amounts of pepper can irritate the esophageal lining and intensify the perception of heartburn in many people with GERD.

## Acidic Foods: Tomato and Citrus

Tomatoes and tomato-based sauces (marinara, ketchup, pizza sauce) are acidic and are a frequent reflux trigger. Citrus fruits and juices similarly increase acid load and can aggravate symptoms.

## Caffeine, Coffee, Chocolate, and Mint

Caffeine and coffee (including decaf, to a lesser degree), chocolate, and peppermint can each relax the lower esophageal sphincter and promote reflux.

## Gentler Choices

Lean grilled or baked proteins, oatmeal, rice, bananas, melons, and non-citrus vegetables are usually better tolerated. Smaller, earlier meals and avoiding lying down soon after eating also reduce reflux.
