# Gentle Low-FODMAP Foods — The Safe Base

Alongside the foods that trigger IBS symptoms, the Monash University framework identifies a broad set of foods that are low in FODMAPs and generally well tolerated. These form a dependable base to build meals around — though, as always, portion size still counts.

## Low-FODMAP Grains and Starches

Plain rice, oats, potato, and quinoa are staple low-FODMAP carbohydrates in the Monash University framework and are usually well tolerated by people with IBS. Rice in particular is a reliable base because it carries essentially none of the fructans that make wheat and rye problematic, and oats are low-FODMAP in normal servings. Naturally gluten-free grains such as corn (maize), buckwheat, and millet round out the options. These starches provide the bulk of a meal without the oligosaccharide load of wheat-based staples. The usual portion caveat applies: very large servings of some of these — oats and sweet potato among them — can climb higher, so moderate helpings keep them firmly in the gentle range.

## Gentle Vegetables

Many vegetables are low in FODMAPs and make up the safe core of a plate. Carrot, cucumber, zucchini (courgette), green beans, bell pepper (capsicum), lettuce, tomato, spinach, and the green tops of spring onions are commonly cited low-FODMAP vegetables in the Monash University framework. These contrast with the higher-FODMAP vegetables that carry fructans (onion, leek bulb) or mannitol (mushroom, cauliflower). Choosing from the gentle list lets a cook add colour, fibre, and flavour without stacking FODMAPs. Portion still matters for some — a small serving of certain otherwise-gentle vegetables is lower than a very large one — but as a group these are the vegetables least likely to provoke symptoms in an ordinary serving.

## Lean Proteins and Eggs

Plain proteins are naturally very low in FODMAPs because FODMAPs are carbohydrates, and unprocessed meat, poultry, fish, seafood, and eggs contain essentially none. In the Monash University framework these are dependable low-FODMAP choices, so chicken, fish, and eggs form a gentle protein base. The caution is with what is added rather than the protein itself: marinades, sauces, breadcrumb coatings, sausages, and pre-seasoned or processed products frequently contain garlic, onion, wheat, or other high-FODMAP ingredients. Firm tofu and tempeh extend the low-FODMAP protein options for plant-based eating. Keeping the protein plain — grilled, baked, or poached without a high-FODMAP sauce — is the way to preserve its gentle status.

## Low-FODMAP Fruit

Not all fruit is high in FODMAPs; several are gentle in ordinary servings. Monash University identifies fruits with a balanced glucose-to-fructose ratio and low polyol content — such as banana (firm and just-ripe), orange and other citrus, strawberry, blueberry, grape, kiwi, and pineapple — as low-FODMAP choices, in contrast to the excess-fructose apple, pear, and mango or the sorbitol-rich stone fruits. Portion is part of the picture: a single serving of a gentle fruit is low, but eating several servings at once raises the total, and ripeness can shift the verdict, as with a firm versus an overripe banana. Sticking to a normal serving of a listed low-FODMAP fruit is the reliable approach.

## Fats, Lactose-Free Dairy, and Building a Base

Fats and oils are essentially FODMAP-free, since FODMAPs are carbohydrates, so olive oil, butter, and plain cooking fats do not add to the load — and garlic-infused oil provides allium flavour without fructans. On the dairy side, the FODMAP of concern is lactose, so lactose-free milk and yogurt, along with hard aged cheeses such as cheddar and parmesan that are naturally very low in lactose, are generally well tolerated in the Monash University framework. Together these gentle groups — low-FODMAP grains, vegetables, plain proteins, listed fruits, fats, and lactose-free dairy — provide enough range to assemble varied meals while keeping the cumulative FODMAP load under an individual's threshold. Watching portions and added ingredients keeps this base dependable.
