# Lactose Intolerance and Dairy

Lactose intolerance results from reduced lactase enzyme activity, leaving lactose (milk sugar) undigested in the gut where it ferments and draws in water, causing bloating, gas, cramping, and diarrhea.

## High-Lactose Dairy

Milk, ice cream, milkshakes, and soft fresh cheeses are high in lactose and are common triggers for people with lactose intolerance. Large servings concentrate the lactose load and increase symptoms.

## Lower-Lactose Options

Hard, aged cheeses (cheddar, parmesan) and butter contain very little lactose and are often well tolerated. Lactose-free milk and yogurt with live cultures are frequently better tolerated than regular milk because some lactose is already broken down.

## Overlap with IBS

Many people with IBS also report dairy sensitivity. Full-fat dairy can additionally aggravate reflux and contribute to a higher overall risk for those with GERD.
